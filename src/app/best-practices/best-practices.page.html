Best Practices

Essential first aid tips and guidelines designed to ensure safety to handle emergencies effectively.

Wash Hands Before Providing First Aid
Before assisting an injured person, always wash your hands with soap and water or use an alcohol-based hand sanitizer. This minimizes the risk of spreading germs, bacteria, and infections.
  • Why It’s Important: Prevents the transmission of diseases and ensures the safety of both the rescuer and the injured individual.
  • Use gloves if available to provide an extra layer of protection.
 Call for Emergency Help Immediately
In any serious or life-threatening situation, always call your local emergency hotline or contact nearby responders as soon as possible.
  • Why It’s Important: Quick communication with emergency personnel ensures that professional medical help is on its way.
  • Provide accurate information about the location and the nature of the injury to responders.
 Use Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
Wear gloves, masks, or any protective gear before coming in contact with an injured person. Even items like plastic bags can serve as improvised protection.
  • Why It’s Important: PPE helps reduce the risk of infection transmission and contamination, ensuring safety for both the rescuer and the injured individual.
 Control Bleeding Effectively
If someone is bleeding, apply firm, direct pressure to the wound with a clean cloth, towel, or bandage. Elevating the injured limb above heart level can also help reduce blood flow.
  • Why It’s Important: Proper bleeding control is crucial to prevent excessive blood loss and potential life-threatening situations.
 Perform CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation) Correctly
CPR is a life-saving procedure performed on someone who has stopped breathing. Place your hands on the center of their chest and press down firmly and quickly at a rate of 100-120 compressions per minute.
  • Why It’s Important: CPR helps maintain blood circulation and oxygen flow to vital organs until professional medical help arrives.
 Treat Burns Safely
In case of a burn, cool the injured area with running cold water for at least 10 minutes. Avoid using ice, as it can damage the tissue further. Cover the burn with a clean, dry dressing.
  • Why It’s Important: Cooling the burn area quickly minimizes tissue damage and provides initial relief while preventing complications.
 Handle Choking Situations Correctly
If someone is choking, perform the Heimlich Maneuver. Stand behind the person, place your hands on their abdomen, and thrust inward and upward. For infants, use back blows and chest thrusts to clear the airway.
  • Why It’s Important: Prompt intervention can remove the obstruction, restore airflow, and save lives.
 Maintain a Clean Emergency First Aid Kit
Keep a well-stocked first aid kit accessible at homes, workplaces, and vehicles. Include essential items like bandages, antiseptics, gloves, CPR face shields, and painkillers.
  • Why It’s Important: A well-prepared first aid kit ensures that you have the necessary tools to respond to emergencies promptly and effectively, even without professional medical help.
